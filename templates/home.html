Hello!
Mason Pike - Developer
M.A. in Urbanology, spec. Contemporary Continental Philosophy - University of London, Institute in Paris (2019-2021)
Advisors: Anna-Louise Milne, Joanne Brueton
Hi I'm Mason. I'm a guy who is often on the computer. I find it quite enjoyable!
I love to make things! Mostly with code, but sometimes with other things, like words or sounds or wires. I've been coding for a long time as a hobby; mostly hacking Nintendo consoles,learning algorithms, and making tools for music. I did my grad school research when crypto was at its hype peak and I ended up writing a lot about contemporary philosophy and blockchain mathematics. I even got some stuff published in some smaller journals. Sometimes I still write stuff like that and post it over on the blog, but I put stuff up and take it down a lot.
I didn't go to engineering school like I probably should have because I was 18 and attempting to build an ill-fated career as a jazz musician. It was fun! Through a long series of coincidence and happenstance, I ended up with a philosophy scholarship to the University of London's Paris Institute and a management position at a local language school. That was also pretty fun! But after all that, I honestly just missed my computer and wanted to come back to it. And so I did!
I try to stay active in the open-source Rust community when I can. I tend to focus on cartographic or mathematical libraries, as well as SDKs for things like game consoles and older computers. My own projects, while not particularly interesting, are usually either software solutions to hardware problems (like my Arturia MKII step sequencer) or emulators, so I can get a better understanding of the CPUs I like to work with.
